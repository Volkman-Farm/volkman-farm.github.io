It is a fair thing to wonder, and the two do look about the same in a photo. The grocery store does not help, using the words loosely and shelving the little tubs side by side. But sprouts and microgreens are different things, grown different ways, and the difference is most of why we do it the way we do.

Here is the short version. A sprout is a seed you germinate in water and eat whole, hull and root and all, usually after two or three days in a jar on the counter. Nothing but water and warmth, no soil, no light. A microgreen grows up out of soil, under light, for one to two weeks. Then we cut the stem and the little leaves off above the soil line and leave the root behind in the tray. You eat the plant, not the seed.

That gap comes down to three things.

**Soil and light.** Sprouts never see either. They swell in the dark in a wet jar and you harvest before the first real leaves show. A microgreen gets planted in a bed of soil, spends its first days in the dark to root down, the way we described on [seeding day](/blog/seed-to-tray-how-a-crop-starts/), then comes up under the lights in the shed and greens up before we ever cut it.

**Harvest stage.** This is the big one. A sprout is harvested as barely more than a woken-up seed. A microgreen is harvested older, at the point where it has spent the seed's stored energy on a stem and its first leaves. Those first leaves are where the flavor and the color live. It is why a tray of [radish](/greens/radish/) micros bites like a radish while a jar of radish sprouts mostly tastes like crunch.

**What you actually eat.** With sprouts it is the whole thing, hull and root included. With a microgreen you eat what grew above the soil, the part we cut. The root stays behind, and around here that spent tray goes out to the chickens.

There is a food-safety angle here too, and we will be plain about it without overstating. Sprouts spend their whole short life warm and wet, which is exactly the condition bacteria like, and that is why they turn up in recall notices now and then. Microgreens grow in soil and get cut above it, a different setup. We are careful either way, but the two are not the same method, and it is worth knowing the difference.

So, not sprouts. Older, grown in soil, grown in light, cut instead of uprooted. If the only version you have met is the tangle in a jar, a tray of the real thing is a different experience. [Start an order](/order/) and taste it for yourself.

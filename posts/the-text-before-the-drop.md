Every delivery starts with a text. Not an app, not an account, not a tracking link that makes you refresh a map. Just a message, from us, the morning your greens go out. We mentioned it in passing when we wrote about [why we drop to porches](/blog/why-we-deliver-to-porches/). It is worth its own post, because that one text is doing more work than it looks like.

Here is roughly what it says. Good morning, your greens are cut and headed your way, look for them on the porch by such and such time. That is it. No reply needed. If you never text back, the clamshell still lands on your step and your week is set.

But the reply is where it earns its keep.

## Why one text, sent that morning

We send it the morning of, not the night before, because that is when we actually know. The greens get cut first, then packed, then the route gets ordered by who is closest to who. Until the trays are down, an ETA is a guess. Once they are cut, we can tell you a real window, so that is when the text goes.

It also tells you something you cannot see from your kitchen: these were alive an hour ago. The text going out in the morning is the freshness clock starting where you can watch it. A message that says cut this morning means more than a sticker that says fresh.

## What happens when you text back

Say the porch is getting a package today and you would rather we tuck the greens somewhere else. Text back, we will put them where you say. Heading out of town Friday and want to skip the week? Text back, no charge, we pick it up next route. Want to add a second clamshell because you have people coming? Text back, and if we have the trays we bring it.

That is the part a pickup lot cannot do. When you drive to a stall, the transaction ends at the table. When the greens come to you, the conversation keeps going, and over a season it turns into something closer to knowing your grocer than clicking a checkout button. We learn that one street likes an earlier drop, that another has a dog who needs the gate latched, that a certain porch wants a heads-up so a toddler does not find the pea shoots first.

None of that lives in an app. It lives in a thread, one neighbor at a time.

If you are on a route, that text will find you on your day. If you are not yet, [start an order](/order/) and tell us your street. The first thing you will get from us is a good morning.

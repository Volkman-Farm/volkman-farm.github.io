Every variety we grow has its people. Pea tendrils have everyone.

If you are new to microgreens and not sure where to start, start here. Pea tendrils are the one we hand a skeptical kid first, because there is nothing to talk anyone into. They taste like a fresh-shelled English pea, sweet and grassy, the flavor of a snap pea cracked open in spring. No heat, no bite, no funk. Just green and a little sweet. That is why we call them the gateway green. They open the door, and the [beet](/greens/beet/) and [kale](/greens/kale/) come next.

## Why they win people over

Part of it is the crunch. Pea tendrils have real substance, tender but with a snap, closer to a salad green than a garnish. We grow them a little taller than the rest, ten to fourteen days instead of the week most varieties want, because the extra height is where the curl comes from. The tendrils twist and coil on their own. A handful on a plate looks like something a chef arranged, and often it is. Pea is the variety the restaurants on our route ask for by name.

They carry more than looks, too. Pea tendrils are higher in protein than most greens, and they are high in vitamin C, vitamin A, and folate, with some potassium and fiber besides. We will not oversell any of that. It is a green, you eat it, it is good for you the way greens are.

## How we eat them at our house

Raw, mostly. They are tender enough to be the salad itself, not just the thing on top. A big pile with lemon and olive oil, and dinner has a side. When we cook with them we add them last, off the heat: folded into risotto in the final minute, or dropped on a bowl of ramen so they wilt but do not cook. Heat is not their friend. They want to stay bright.

If you have kids who eye a salad with suspicion, this is the tray to bring home. Ours will graze pea tendrils straight out of the [clamshell](/blog/why-we-deliver-to-porches/) before it ever reaches a plate, which is fine by us. Better in them than wilting in the drawer.

Ten varieties come off our shelves at $3.50 an ounce, and folks argue about which is best. We will not settle that here. But if you only try one, and especially if you are bringing along someone who swears they do not like greens, [start with pea](/greens/pea/).

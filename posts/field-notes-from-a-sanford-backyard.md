Five of us live on this piece of Sanford. Two parents, three kids, and a shed out back that grows [ten varieties of microgreens](/greens/) for neighbors across the Orlando metro. That part you may already know.

Here is the part we have not written down anywhere: the shed was never the whole plan. It was the first working piece of something bigger. We want this yard to feed us, teach the kids, and waste as little as possible. Some people call that homesteading. The design ideas we keep borrowing come from permaculture. We are early. The honest word for most of the yard right now is potential.

So we are starting a journal, and we are writing it in public.

## What we'll write about

**Microgreens, most of all.** They are the working farm and the thing we know best. Expect variety spotlights, what to actually do with an ounce of radish greens on a Tuesday, how a tray goes from seed to your porch, and what happens on a delivery morning.

**The homestead, as it really is.** Not the finished version. The version where we are still mapping shade, starting the first compost pile, and learning what Florida sand does to a garden plan. When something dies in July, we will tell you it died in July.

**Permaculture, without the seminar.** Work with the yard you have. Catch the rain where it falls. Let one system feed the next. We already run one small loop: spent material from the grow trays goes to compost instead of the trash. We want more loops like that, and we will document each one as we build it.

## Why bother writing it down

Three reasons.

First, our customers keep asking. When we hand a bag over on a porch in [Lake Mary](/delivery/lake-mary/) or [Sanford](/delivery/sanford/), the questions are rarely about price. They are about how the greens grow, what the shed looks like, whether the kids really help. (They do. Packing day has a full crew.)

Second, writing forces honesty. A garden plan in your head is always thriving. A garden plan on the page has dates and results. We would rather be accountable to a record than to a memory.

Third, Central Florida deserves better gardening notes. Most homestead advice online is written for somewhere with seasons and soil. Down here fall is planting season, summer is for cover crops and patience, and the ground is sand. We will write for the place we actually live.

## The rhythm

We plan to post most days, each day with its own lane: a variety spotlight to start the week, kitchen ideas, the homestead journal, a permaculture note, a dispatch from the delivery route, growing notes from the shed, and reader questions on Sundays.

If you have a question you want answered in that Sunday slot, ask on the porch or reply to a delivery text. We will get to it.

And if you have never tried the greens that started all this, that part is easy. [Start an order](/order/) and we will hand you the freshest thing you eat all week.

See you tomorrow.

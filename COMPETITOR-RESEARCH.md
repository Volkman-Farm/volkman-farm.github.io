# Competitor Research: Microgreens, Local and Best-in-Class

Research date: 2026-05-23. Method: WebSearch plus WebFetch against each live site. Sites that refused fetch (ECONNREFUSED, 403) are noted as such; for those, signals are reconstructed from SERP snippets and partner directories, and are flagged as lower-confidence.

---

## Part 1. Local Orlando-area competitors

### 1. Kyrios Farm Microgreens (kyriosfarm.com)

One-line positioning: Family-owned vertical farm in Lake Mary delivering microgreens "where freshness meets flavor" to Seminole and Orange County, with a dedicated chef channel.

SEO signals
- Title: `Fresh Microgreens in Orlando | Local & Sustainable | Kyrios Farm` (62 chars, well constructed, geo-keyword first).
- H1: `Local Microgreens. Where Freshness Meets Flavor!` (weak; no city in H1).
- Heading structure: H1 then a jump to H6s. They are skipping H2 and H3 entirely. That is a real on-page SEO error.
- Schema: None observable on the home delivery page. No LocalBusiness, no Product, no FAQ. They are leaving free Google real estate on the table.
- Internal linking: Has a dedicated `/microgreens-home-delivery-orlando` URL and a `/microgreens-our-farm` URL. No per-city pages (no Lake Mary page, no Sanford page).
- Content depth: Home, Chefs, Products, Learn, About, Contact, Blog. The Blog and Learn exist, depth unverified but at least the slots are built.
- Indexable pages: roughly 15-25, Wix default sitemap.

Design signals
- Wix build. Default Wix font stack (sans-serif). No distinctive typography.
- Color palette: mostly white background with green accents and product photo color. No identifiable brand color discipline.
- Photography: a mix of real farm photography (operational shots with file names like `IMG20220520200429`) and product close-ups. Looks like phone photography uploaded without art direction.
- Hero: full-width banner, tagline plus dual CTAs ("For Chefs" and "Order Now"). Standard Wix template feel.
- Navigation: 7 items across the top. One too many.

Conversion / business
- Pricing: hidden on the home page; visible on the subscriptions page ($6 for 2oz portions, $20 sampler). 2oz at $6 is ~$3/oz, slightly cheaper than Volkman.
- Order mechanism: Wix native cart with a prepaid subscription model. Minimum $20 for free shipping, $5 fee otherwise.
- Subscription: yes, prepaid weekly or biweekly. The model is the opposite of Volkman's "pay on delivery, skip any week."
- Wholesale: dedicated "For Chefs" page in primary nav.
- Social proof: none visible on home page. No reviews, no chef logos.

Notable
- This is the strongest local competitor by SEO surface area: they own the `kyriosfarm.com/microgreens-home-delivery-orlando` URL and rank for "microgreens Orlando." But the page has no schema and the H1 hierarchy is broken. A well-structured Volkman home delivery page could outrank it.

## A. The 5 to 7 best things observed

### 1. Per-city or per-zone landing pages with explicit town lists
- **Site that does it:** Fields of Revery names every town they deliver to (Clover, Lake Wylie, Fort Mill, Rock Hill, Charlotte, Gastonia) on the home page and in dedicated copy. Kyrios Farm has `/microgreens-home-delivery-orlando` as a unique URL.
- **Why it works:** It captures both branded ("Fields of Revery delivery") and unbranded ("microgreens Fort Mill") search at the same time, and chef/restaurant customers can verify route fit in one glance.
- **How Volkman could adapt it:** Volkman already has nine city pages (`/delivery/{slug}/`) per the index.md. The unlock is making sure each one is fully built, has its own H1 ("Microgreens delivered in Lake Mary, Friday morning"), and is internally linked from the home page (which it already is). One afternoon of work.

### 2. Cultivar-specific naming on variety pages
- **Site that does it:** Hortus Valley Farm names "Pea Microgreens - Dundale," "Broccoli Microgreens - Di Cicco," "Cabbage Microgreens - Green." Each is a distinct URL.
- **Why it works:** Long-tail keyword capture ("dundale pea microgreens"), credibility signal to chefs who recognize the variety, and Google understands them as distinct Products in schema.
- **How Volkman could adapt it:** Volkman's variety page list is generic ("arugula, beet, broccoli, cilantro..."). Naming the actual cultivar where it matters (e.g., "Hong Vit Radish Microgreens," "Red Russian Kale Microgreens") lifts the variety pages from "everyone has this page" to "this is the only page for this cultivar." Half a day of copy work per variety, ten varieties.

### 3. Audience-segmented navigation (Residential / Restaurant / Retail)
- **Site that does it:** Mountain Man Micro Farms has distinct ordering destinations for Retail, Restaurant, and Residential users.
- **Why it works:** A chef and a home cook are not the same reader. Forcing them through the same page costs both. Splitting the path lets the wholesale tone be wholesale and the residential tone be neighborly.
- **How Volkman could adapt it:** Volkman already has `/restaurants/` doing the wholesale split. The opportunity is making the home-page split crisper. Two CTAs side by side: "Order for my house" and "Order for my restaurant," each with its own visual treatment per the wholesale restraint rule in BRAND.md. One afternoon.

### 4. Recipe-and-usage content as a long-tail SEO asset
- **Site that does it:** Mighty Greens Houston has a Recipe Ideas page with six categories. Fields of Revery names use cases ("salads, sandwiches, wraps, smoothies, protein dishes, pasta, tacos") even without recipes.
- **Why it works:** "How to use microgreens" is one of the highest-volume long-tail searches in this category. Catching it without writing a food blog is achievable: name the use cases on each variety page in a "How to use" section (which Volkman already plans, per index.md).
- **How Volkman could adapt it:** Strengthen the "How to use" H2 on each variety page with three to five specific applications. Not a recipe blog. A scannable list of plate-level uses ("scatter on tacos, fold into eggs, top a grain bowl"). Half a day per variety.

### 5. Founder presence with name, not face
- **Site that does it:** Mighty Greens Houston opens the about with "My name is Ariel, your local microgreens farmer." Harvest Greens Farm centers Kelly Lauterbach.
- **Why it works:** A single named human is the cheapest, most durable trust signal a food business owns. It is not an Instagram trick, it does not require a face, and it is the right answer for "is this real."
- **How Volkman could adapt it:** Volkman is the named human on a brand-fits short About. BRAND.md already encodes this. The unlock is putting "Albert built the shed" on the About page (the BRAND.md rewrite already does this). No photo required.

### 6. Pricing visible on the page (not gated behind contact)
- **Site that does it:** Fields of Revery shows $5-$9 per microgreens product. Brickell shows $11.75-$18.75. Bardmoor shows $6 standard, $20 for Rainbow Beets.
- **Why it works:** A hidden price implies a premium that has to be justified. A visible price ends the friction before the customer leaves the page. Volkman is already doing this ($3.50/oz) and that is a quiet win.
- **How Volkman could adapt it:** Keep doing it. Resist the urge to add subscription-only pricing tiers that obscure the unit price.

### 7. Real human testimonials with attribution
- **Site that does it:** Fields of Revery has six named testimonials including a chef. Plant Prana has UGC-style review overlays. Bardmoor has Alex B. praising "professional packaging, end-to-end transparency, and the best tasting greens."
- **Why it works:** A first name + a city + a one-line specific quote outperforms five-star generic praise. Restaurant logos are even better.
- **How Volkman could adapt it:** Volkman has no testimonials on the site today. Adding three named neighbor quotes ("Friday is the day the greens show up. Best part of the week. — Jamie, Lake Mary") and one chef quote when one becomes available is a one-afternoon task. The BRAND.md blockquote component already exists for this. Use it.

## B. The 3 to 5 worst things observed

### 1. Wellness-brand voice with medical adjacents
- **Site that does it badly:** Mico's Micro Farm ("doctor-designed, nutrient-dense blends...targeted microgreens for specific health outcomes"). Plant Prana Wellness ("your health is your most valuable asset").
- **Why it hurts them:** Bordering medical-claim copy reads as marketing-first to anyone who reads carefully. It also invites regulatory exposure that BRAND.md explicitly avoids. And the language is interchangeable with every supplement brand, which dilutes the local-food story.
- **What Volkman should learn:** Stay on the BRAND.md banned-words list. No "superfood," no "nutrient-dense" as a positioning word (mentioning sulforaphane content is fine, leading with the claim is not).

### 2. Stock or placeholder imagery used as final photography
- **Site that does it badly:** Tampa Bay Sprouting Co. has transparent-GIF placeholders sitting in production. Health Is Wealth Micro Farms similarly has placeholder GIFs. Sunshine Sprouts' site appears mostly unfinished.
- **Why it hurts them:** A real photo of your actual product is the lowest bar a food website can clear. Failing it is the strongest signal a customer gets that the operation is not serious.
- **What Volkman should learn:** Follow DESIGN-SYSTEM.md section 6 literally. Shoot the hero, the about pair, and ten variety photos with one window and one phone. The standard is "one family took these," not "studio strobe."

### 3. Generic Wix template with broken H1 hierarchy
- **Site that does it badly:** Kyrios Farm jumps from H1 to H6 with no H2/H3/H4/H5 between. Plant Prana does this less severely but inconsistently.
- **Why it hurts them:** Screen readers cannot navigate the page properly, and Google reads the structure as a signal of low information density. It is the kind of error every Wix template emits unless the owner explicitly fixes it.
- **What Volkman should learn:** Volkman is on Eleventy with hand-written templates. Section 1 H1, section 2 H2, sub-section H3, never deeper. The DESIGN-SYSTEM.md already encodes this. Audit before publishing.

### 4. Phone-only or DM-only order paths with no on-page form
- **Site that does it badly:** Orlando Microgreens runs on email and Facebook. Tiny Leaf Micro Green Farm requires WhatsApp or messenger for orders. Harvest Greens Farm requires submitting contact details for "personalized outreach."
- **Why it hurts them:** Every step between "I want greens" and "order placed" is a customer dropped. A customer who has to email is a customer who often does not.
- **What Volkman should learn:** Keep the on-page progressive form on `/microgreens/`. The four-step inline pattern in DESIGN-SYSTEM.md is the correct answer. Do not regress to a contact form.

### 5. Subscription-only models that hide the unit price
- **Site that does it badly:** Brickell Micro Farms gates pricing inside subscription tier dropdowns ($11.75-$18.75 per 60g, but the customer has to math to compare). Kyrios Farm requires committing to a subscription plus a $5 fee under $20.
- **Why it hurts them:** A weekly subscription is a real ask. A customer who cannot see the unit price and cannot opt out cleanly will not start the trial.
- **What Volkman should learn:** "Standing weekly orders get 10% off. Skip any week, cancel anytime. No card on file." That copy is already on the home page. It is the strongest line in the whole comp set on this dimension. Do not change it.

## C. The 3 sharpest SEO wins available to Volkman Farm

### 1. Own the per-city SERPs that nobody else is building for
- **Where competitors are weak:** Not one local Orlando competitor has a dedicated per-city URL. Kyrios has a single Orlando home delivery page. Hortus lists cities as body copy. Orlando Microgreens, Sunshine Sprouts, Health Is Wealth, Central Florida Greens, Plant Prana: zero city pages between them.
- **Volkman's move:** Volkman already has `/delivery/lake-mary/`, `/delivery/sanford/`, `/delivery/winter-springs/`, `/delivery/oviedo/`, `/delivery/casselberry/`, `/delivery/maitland/`, `/delivery/winter-park/`, `/delivery/college-park/`, and `/delivery/downtown-orlando/` per the home page. Make sure each one has a unique H1 (city name + service), a unique 60-word intro, the delivery day for that city, the standard variety list, and a CTA. Add LocalBusiness schema with `areaServed` listing all nine cities. This is the single sharpest SEO win available, and the structural work is already done.
- **Shape:** one afternoon to audit, one afternoon to add schema. Probably ships in a week.

### 2. FAQ schema on the home page, the pricing page, and the restaurants page
- **Where competitors are weak:** Nobody in the local set has FAQPage JSON-LD. Plant Prana has a FAQ page but not the schema. Mountain Man has FAQ content without schema. The result: nobody is earning the FAQ rich result in the local SERP.
- **Volkman's move:** Five questions on the home page ("When do you deliver in my neighborhood?" "How do you pay?" "Do you do restaurants?" "What if I want to skip a week?" "Where are the greens cut?"), each with a short text answer, all wrapped in FAQPage JSON-LD. Same shape on the pricing and restaurants pages with different questions.
- **Shape:** one afternoon. The content already exists in the BRAND.md voice; the work is wrapping it in JSON-LD and validating in Google's Rich Results Test.

### 3. Cultivar-specific Product schema on variety pages
- **Where competitors are weak:** Hortus Valley Farm gets default Shopify Product schema but does not differentiate by cultivar in a way Google can read (they use Title Case names but no `additionalProperty` for cultivar). Every other local competitor has zero Product schema.
- **Volkman's move:** On each `/greens/{slug}/`, add JSON-LD Product schema with `name`, `description`, `offers.price` ($3.50), `offers.priceCurrency`, `brand`, and a `cultivar` or `varietyName` `additionalProperty`. The page already exists per DESIGN-SYSTEM.md Variety landing archetype.
- **Shape:** half a day. One Liquid include that takes the variety frontmatter and emits the schema.

## D. The 3 sharpest design moves to consider

### 1. A "what we deliver this week" line, treated as a stamped notice
- **Pattern source:** Closest reference is Mountain Man Micro Farms' `Fresh and on your schedule` declarative tagline, plus the Fields of Revery testimonial-list cadence. Neither does exactly this, but both gesture at it.
- **Why it translates to Volkman:** A small line near the hero stating the current cut list ("This week: arugula, broccoli, pea tendril, radish, Swiss chard") gives the page a freshness signal that does not require new photography and reinforces the "alive this morning" line above it.
- **BRAND.md alignment:** Supports section 1 ("we grow ten kinds of microgreens, cut them the morning we deliver them") and section 4 word-choices ("cut," "morning," "ten varieties"). Implementable as a small `cta-row`-styled component per DESIGN-SYSTEM.md section 3.
- **Shape:** an afternoon, plus a recurring 5-minute weekly edit. The Volkman family probably writes this in three minutes from the harvest list.

### 2. Audience-segmented dual CTA on the home page
- **Pattern source:** Mountain Man's three-path nav (Retail / Restaurant / Residential), reduced to two paths for Volkman.
- **Why it translates:** Volkman's home page already has two CTAs at the top ("Place an order," "See prices"). Replacing those with `For my kitchen` and `For my restaurant`, each linking to its own destination (`/microgreens/` and `/restaurants/`), makes the audience split structural rather than incidental, and uses the existing two-CTA pattern.
- **BRAND.md alignment:** Supports decision 10.3 ("Wholesale visual identity. Distinct"). The two CTAs can carry slightly different visual weight, with the residential one being the sprout-green primary and the restaurant one being the more restrained sprout-deep secondary text link. DESIGN-SYSTEM.md section 3 already has the CTA row pattern.
- **Shape:** one afternoon of copy edit and CSS tweak. Ships immediately.

### 3. Testimonial blockquote, used sparingly, in the existing component
- **Pattern source:** Fields of Revery, Bardmoor, Plant Prana, Mighty Greens Houston all do this. Fields of Revery does it best: named, short, specific, chef quote included.
- **Why it translates:** DESIGN-SYSTEM.md already builds the blockquote component (Fraunces italic, 4px sprout left border, optional `<cite>`). It is the only component in the system not yet used on any page. Wiring up a single neighbor quote on the home page and a single chef quote on the restaurants page (when one arrives) costs nothing structurally.
- **BRAND.md alignment:** Supports section 3 ("Confident, never proud"). A neighbor quoting us is more credible than us claiming we are confident. The wholesale page testimonial is explicitly anticipated in DESIGN-SYSTEM.md section 9, step 14.
- **Shape:** an afternoon, once the family asks two neighbors and one chef for one sentence each. The text-message ask is fifteen seconds. The CSS is already built.

A note on what I did not recommend: Fields of Revery's lowercase sans-serif headline treatment is design-pretty but conflicts with DESIGN-SYSTEM.md typography (Fraunces 600 with `-0.01em` letter-spacing, title-cased H1). Mighty Greens Houston's mixed-case keyword-shouting H1 conflicts with BRAND.md section 4 sentence rules. Mico's pastel aesthetic conflicts with the cream foundation in BRAND.md section 5. None of those are good fits.

## E. The leaderboard, 5 sites ranked

1. **Fields of Revery (fieldsofrevery.com).** Best balance of warm voice, real commerce, named delivery zones, and educational depth. Closest spirit-cousin to Volkman in palette and customer model. The site Volkman could most credibly aim to outclass within its smaller geography.

2. **Mountain Man Micro Farms (mtmanfarms.com).** Best information architecture in the set. The Retail/Restaurant/Residential split is the clearest answer to a two-audience site, and the Food Safety page is a chef-credibility move nobody else has.

3. **Mighty Greens Houston (mightygreenshouston.com).** Best content surface in the set. Recipe Ideas, Testimonials, Referral program, Loyalty, Gift Cards, Pre-order. Excessive for a one-family operation, but the Recipe Ideas page is the model for a "How to use" section that compounds in SEO.

4. **Brickell Micro Farms (brickellmicrofarms.com).** Best transactional commerce. Visible per-pack prices, subscription discount math, Google review embeds, restaurant testimonials, and a clear delivery-zone commitment to Miami. The voice is too tech-modern for Volkman, but the e-commerce hygiene is solid.

5. **Kyrios Farm (kyriosfarm.com).** Best of the local Orlando set, primarily on SEO surface area. They have the home-delivery URL, the chefs URL, the our-farm URL, the blog, and the title-tag fundamentals. Their H1 hierarchy is broken and their schema is absent, which is precisely why Volkman can leapfrog them within a month.

Fresh Origins, Plant Prana, Bardmoor, Tampa Bay Sprouting, Tiny Leaf, Mico's, Health Is Wealth, Central Florida Greens, Hortus Valley Farm, Sunshine Sprouts, Orlando Microgreens, Beringer Farms, Harvest Greens Farm, and Urban Roots Farms are below the leaderboard either because they are not direct comparables (Fresh Origins, Urban Roots), because the site is functionally incomplete (Sunshine Sprouts, Health Is Wealth, Tampa Bay Sprouting), or because the operating model is too far from Volkman to inform design (Mico's, Plant Prana wellness frame).
